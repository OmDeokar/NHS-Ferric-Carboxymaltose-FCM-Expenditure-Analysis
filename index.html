Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive analysis of Ferric Carboxymaltose (FCM) intravenous iron injection utilisation and expenditure across NHS England secondary care trusts, covering April 2019 to January 2026 — 82 months of validated and provisional data. Using the NHS Secondary Care Medicines Data (SCMD), a total of 21,431 clean dispensing records were analysed across 174 NHS trusts in all seven NHS England regions.

Total NHS indicative expenditure on FCM over the study period reached £235.5 million. London accounted for the largest regional share (28.8%, £67.9M), followed by Midlands (20.1%, £47.2M). A consistent upward trend was observed across both cost and utilisation, with a single structural break in early 2020 caused by COVID-19 elective care suspension.

Among eight forecasting models evaluated with walk-forward cross-validation, Prophet achieved the best performance (MAPE 3.60%), projecting continued FCM utilisation of 22,000–27,000 vials per month through 2026/27.

Cost Methodology: All spend figures are indicative costs based on NHS dm+d list prices as reported in SCMD (quantity × dm+d unit price). Actual NHS procurement costs are typically 10–30% lower due to negotiated NHS Supply Chain and framework agreements.

Data & Methodology

2.1 Data Sources

Combined (de-duplicated)Apr 2019 – Jan 202621,53521,431✅ Clean
Overlap Resolution: For the 39-month overlap (Apr 2021–Mar 2025), Finalised data was used and 10,534 Provisional rows were dropped to prevent double-counting. Provisional data covers only the 10 most recent months (Apr 2025 – Jan 2026).

2.2 Processing Pipeline

  1. Extraction — Auto-detection and loading of SCMD ZIP archives
  2. Deduplication — Overlap resolution; Finalised prioritised for shared months
  3. Cleaning — 104 invalid rows removed (negative quantities, missing dates)
  4. Enrichment — Trust names (550), NHS Region (3-tier lookup), ICB, brand classification
  5. Feature Engineering — 34 columns: vials, cost/vial, YoY growth, vials/day, seasonality
  6. Summaries — National monthly/annual, 7-region, 174-trust, formulation breakdowns
  7. Forecasting — 8 models, walk-forward CV, randomised hyperparameter tuning (20 iters)

2.3 Regional Mapping

All 174 active NHS trusts were successfully mapped to NHS England regions with zero "Unknown Region" rows. A three-tier lookup was used: (1) direct ODS→Region lookup file (121 mappings), (2) pre-filled region column from trust master CSV, (3) postcode-derived region as fallback.

Figure 1  ·  Monthly Total Spend — Apr 2019 to Jan 2026
Monthly total indicative FCM spend (£M) across all 174 NHS England trusts. The April 2020 dip to £1.1M reflects COVID-19 elective suspension. Source: SCMD Finalised + Provisional.

Monthly FCM expenditure demonstrates a clear upward trajectory, rising from ~£2.1M/month (Apr 2019) to a peak of £3.74M (early 2024). The single most pronounced structural break is the COVID-19 dip of April–May 2020, where spend fell to £1.1M — a 48% collapse reflecting the nationwide suspension of elective surgery and non-urgent IV therapy. Recovery was rapid, with pre-pandemic spend levels restored by Q3 2020.

Key Findings & Implications

Finding 01 — Scale
£235.5M in total indicative spend across 174 trusts over 82 months — a substantial and growing medicines expenditure category in NHS England secondary care.
Finding 02 — Growth
Consistent upward trend: cost grew +44.7% from 2019/20 to 2024/25. COVID-19 (2020) was the only interruption to this unbroken trajectory.
Finding 03 — Geography
London accounts for 28.8% of national spend despite ~15% of England's population — above-average FCM intensity driven by tertiary hospital density.
Finding 04 — Trusts
Manchester University NHS FT is the highest-named trust (~£7.49M). An unresolved Midlands trust (£9.42M) may rank #1 nationally once ODS codes are resolved.
Finding 05 — Forecast
Prophet model achieves MAPE 3.60% — best of 8 models. Projects stable demand of 22,000–27,000 vials/month through 2026/27, with no sharp growth resumption.
Finding 06 — Seasonality
Consistent winter peak (Dec–Feb) across all years supports proactive supply chain planning and pre-winter elective pathway iron optimisation.

Commissioning Implications

The 2024/25 cost plateau (+0.8%) warrants monitoring — it may reflect market maturation, formulary stewardship initiatives, or early biosimilar market entry. London commissioners may wish to review FCM prescribing intensity relative to other regions, which could indicate either clinical need variation or formulary adherence differences.
